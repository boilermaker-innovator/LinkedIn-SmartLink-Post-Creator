Oracle's Early Days
Oracle was founded in 1977 by Larry Ellison to solve the massive problem of business data scattered across incompatible systems.

In the 1970s, businesses struggled with data trapped in incompatible databases and systems. Oracle created the first commercial SQL database, allowing companies to organize and access their information systematically. This solved a universal business problem and created a $100B+ infrastructure company.

Oracle's success demonstrates how solving fundamental information organization problems creates massive value. The parallel to web information chaos suggests similar infrastructure opportunity potential.

Web Information Organization
The systematic arrangement of scattered web content to make information findable, accessible, and useful without context switching.

Currently, web information is fragmented across billions of pages with no systematic organization. Users waste time opening multiple tabs, losing context, and struggling to compare information. This creates a $436B annual productivity loss from research abandonment.

Solving web information organization could impact 2B+ websites vs Oracle's 50M business customers - potentially 40x larger addressable market. First-mover advantage in this space could create the next fundamental web infrastructure layer.
